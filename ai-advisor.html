The five most common mistakes: (1) Trying to do everything at once — pick one problem, solve it with AI, then expand. (2) Not reviewing AI output — AI makes mistakes, especially with specific local details. Always have a human review customer-facing content. (3) Choosing tools based on hype — the most-advertised AI tool is rarely the best fit for a local service business. Match the tool to the specific problem. (4) Abandoning it too soon — AI tools improve as they learn your business. Give it 60 to 90 days before judging results. (5) Ignoring training — spend one hour getting comfortable with a new tool before deploying it with customers. That hour pays for itself immediately.
Measure what matters before you start so you have a baseline. Key metrics to track: number of leads captured per week, percentage of calls answered, time spent on administrative tasks per day, customer review count and average rating, and revenue per month. After 60 to 90 days with AI tools in place, compare those numbers. Most businesses see the clearest ROI in leads captured outside business hours (AI phone answering) and time saved on repetitive writing tasks. If an AI tool costs $100/month and saves you five hours per week, and your time is worth $30/hour, that's $520/month in value against $100 in cost. The math is usually very clear.
